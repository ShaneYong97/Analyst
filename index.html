Identify and Monitor a Stock's Critical Factors

Equity Research
▼

Definition of a critical factor: Occur within investment horizon. Have a clear catalyst. Causes Material change in growth, corporate event, and volatility profile. EPS or Cash flow per share of 5%. ROIC or ROE of 50bps.

How to find critical factor: Compare relative valuation level & financial metrics of the stock against index & peers. Compute CAGR of financial metrics over the years. Dupont Analysis. Current assumptions critical to current consensus. Oversimplified investment thesis.

Catalysts are usually found in: Company sponsored analyst meeting and calls. Earnings release. Company's annual pricing, volume, earnings guidance or projection. Deadlines for new legislation. Pre-scheduled announcements by company's customers, competitors and suppliers. New Product releases. Interim sales data for the company or sector.

Costco Wholesale Corporation (NASDAQ: COST) — Equity Research Report

Base case assume limited impact from the geopolitical tension, Costco grows at a slightly faster pace, steady margin, and dividend payment increasing steadily at 11% per year. In this case, EPS is expected to grow 11.42% and valuation maintains, resulting in price target of $1,062.39 for FY end 2026, potential upside of 5.07%.

Bear case considers potential increase in cost to upkeep CAPEX due to geopolitical tension and decrease in dividend payout, in which case earnings can take a hit and valuation retract towards its previous level of 37.5x P/E. An immediate retraction is unlikely but a retraction to 45x P/E is a reasonable level, resulting in an implied price of $895.50 for FY end 2026, potential downside of 11.43%.
