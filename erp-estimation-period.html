The optimal time period for the estimation of equity risk premium

Equity Risk Premium

For the estimation of equity risk premium, a longer time period generally leads to a lower standard error, and hence a more reliable and less biased measure. However one question that surfaces is ‘does the perception of equity risk in 1928 remain the same with what we’re looking at now?’ In fact the equity risk premium estimated using the period from 1928 to year 1942 is a negative figure, which does not make sense in today’s world considering the facility and the regulations we have today. The challenge of finding the equity risk premium that reflects how market is perceiving equity risk today is finding the right balance between using a long enough period to reach a reasonable standard error, and using a updated enough period to represent the idea of risk perceived by market today.

Reference: Damodaran, Aswath, Equity Risk Premiums (ERP): Determinants, Estimates and Implications - The 2026 Edition (March 05, 2026). Available at SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=6361419 or http://dx.doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6361419
