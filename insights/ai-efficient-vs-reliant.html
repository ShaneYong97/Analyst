AI Efficient v.s. AI Reliant

AI & Productivity

After being skeptical of AI usage in research work for the longest time, I could no longer turn a blind eye after the huge breakthrough of Anthropic Mythos, and after I gave it a try, boy oh boy I can definitely see how it’s going to change how we work. What used to be infeasible due to time constraint can now be done in a day, it is an understatement saying that it has saved me 50% of my time in my research routine.

However we have to be mindful of how we use AI and beware of the risk of overreliance of AI and inaccuracy of our work. Learn how to communicate with AI, train your AI to be prudent and prioritise fact integrity because that dictates the accuracy of your work. Most importantly, draw a line between task that is too important to rely on AI and task that can be delegated to boost efficiency.

With that out of the way, the first thing I did was solve a problem that had been bugging me for a while, staying on top of market insights without spending half my day watching videos and podcasts. I built an automated system that pulls transcripts from my subscribed channels to spit out a clean daily report every morning, and honestly it feels like having a research assistant working overnight. From there I got more ambitious. I put together a fully automated macro dashboard using VBA to track key economic indicators so I can see everything in one place, something I’d always wanted but never had the bandwidth to build. Lastly, I have had AI help me build a website to document my journey and share my work.

It is an exciting process that I get to reshape my routine with the vastly expanded possibilities provided by the help of AI, and I cannot imagine how fast the gap is going to widen between people who can utilise AI and people who don’t 1 year down the road.
